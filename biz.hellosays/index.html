Feature guide
A full tour of every feature with real user scenarios
This guide walks through every screen and feature in the HelloSays Business App, in the same order they appear in your sidebar. Each section explains what the feature does and walks through a real scenario showing how a plumber would actually use it. Click any sidebar item to jump to the designed UI for that feature.
Free features — available to all claimed businesses
1Home dashboard
What it does

The default landing screen for every claimed business. Shows four key metrics at a glance: Impressions (how many times your listing appeared in search), Page Visits (how many consumers viewed your profile), Leads (contact button clicks from your listing), and Average Response Time (how fast you reply to customer inquiries). Below the metrics is a welcome banner and a teal upgrade CTA promoting AI calling, SMS, and lead management. The bottom "Insights" section has a 12-month bar chart comparing your page visits against top competitors, plus a list of the top 3 nearby competitors with their review counts, star ratings, and distance from your location. This is the one screen free-tier users see the most — it's designed to show value while consistently reminding them that upgrading unlocks the real business tools.

Real scenario

David just claimed his plumbing business on HelloSays.com last week. Monday morning, he opens the app for the first time to see how things are going.

1David lands on the Home screen. The four metric cards show: 0 Impressions (last month, before claim), 23 Page Visits, 10 Leads, and a green "4m" average response time with "Under 5m target" badge.
2A pink welcome banner reads "Welcome to HelloSays for business Owners — make sure your page is looking great and check insights below." David clicks through it to verify his business info.
3Below that, a dark teal gradient banner catches his eye: "Unlock AI calling, lead management & SMS — capture every missed call. Plans from $99/mo." He's intrigued but not ready yet.
4He scrolls to the Insights chart: "Page visits: you vs competitors" showing 6 months of bar data. His bars (in coral) are trending up in the last 3 months, while his competitors' (in light pink) are flatter.
5The Top Competitors card shows Bernal's Service Bays (★★★★★, 51 reviews, 3.5 km away), Quick Lane Tire (★★★★★, 65 reviews, 5.4 km), and Tire Mart (★★★★, 51 reviews, 5.4 km).
6David sees his competitors have way more reviews. He decides to upgrade to Starter so he can start using AI calling and build a bigger review base through review request automation.
2Business Info
What it does

The business profile page where plumbers manage everything consumers see on their HelloSays listing. The main column has four editable category cards (Emergency Plumbing, Drain & Sewer Service, Water Heaters, Fixtures & Installation), each with pill tags listing specific services. There's a detailed Hours of Operation table with Mon-Sun rows, an extended closures banner for temporary shutdowns, special hours for holidays, and a "From This Business" section for adding the company's history and founder intro. The right rail has three cards: a "Set Up Your Page" prompt with an illustration, an interactive location card showing address/phone/website with a map pin, and a HelloSays 24/7 Customer Support card. Every category card has an Edit button and a coral top border that matches the existing live product design.

6Leads & schedule (3 views + Week/Month + time filter)
What it does

The central workspace for managing all leads. A collapsible "Needs scheduling" panel on the left (280px) shows every unscheduled lead grouped by urgency — Emergency at top in red, then New, then Contacted — no matter which view you're in, so pending leads never disappear from sight. A toggle button in the header (panel icon) hides or shows the panel with a smooth animation; when hidden, the panel's icon turns coral and shows a count badge of unscheduled leads so users are reminded that action items still need attention. The main area has three switchable views via icon buttons in the top-right: Kanban (pipeline board with 5 columns: New → Contacted → Booked → Completed → Lost), Calendar (toggleable between Month and Week sub-views), and Graph (conversion funnel showing Total → Contacted → Booked → Completed with drop-off percentages, revenue totals, and insights). Calendar is the default view. Within Calendar, a Week/Month toggle in the header lets users switch between two paradigms: Month view (a 6-week grid showing appointments as floating cards with colored top bars inside each date cell, technician initial avatars, and time pills; great for seeing how booked the month is at a glance) or Week view (a Mon-Sun × 7 AM-7 PM hour-block grid with appointment cards matching the Month view's design language — white card, top urgency bar, tech avatar, time pill; great for operational scheduling of specific times). A global time period dropdown in the header (Today / This week / This month / Last 30 days / This quarter / All time) filters all 3 views at once: calendar range updates, Kanban lead counts update, Graph funnel counts/percentages/bar widths recalculate and the revenue and close rate shift accordingly. Each view serves a different question: "what's my pipeline?" (Kanban), "how booked is my month?" (Calendar Month), "what am I doing this week?" (Calendar Week), "how well am I converting?" (Graph). On mobile, Kanban stays as a horizontally-scrolling 5-column grid, Calendar Week switches to a single-day view with day pills, Month view compacts cards but keeps the grid intact, and Graph stacks the funnel bars vertically.

7Lead detail & communication timeline
What it does

The full detail view for a single lead, opened by tapping any lead card. The header shows customer name, urgency badge, phone number, and two action buttons (Mark contacted, Book job). The left side has the Communication Timeline — every interaction in reverse-chronological order: AI calls with duration and play button, inbound SMS, outbound SMS from the system or plumber, web form submissions, and internal notes (coral-tinted cards visible to team but hidden from customer). The right side has a Plumbing Diagnostic panel showing structured data the AI captured or the plumber added (Job Type, Urgency, Pipe Material, Shut-off Valve, Water Pressure) plus a Notes card for team annotations.

10Workflows
What it does

The SMS workflow editor. A left column shows the visual flow of a workflow: a teal-blocked trigger (e.g. "Missed call") at the top, connected via thin lines to numbered step boxes. Standard steps show a step number, an action type (Send SMS, Detect intent, Wait), timing info, and the message template. The right column is the Step Editor — whichever step is clicked in the flow shows here. Editable fields: message textarea, variable chips ({name}, {business_name}, {eta}), timing dropdown, skip-if checkboxes (Opted out, Sent in last 1h). Top buttons: Test, Save & activate.
